Question: Explain Ohm's Law and calculate the current when V = 12V and R = 4 ohms.
Total: 6 marks.

Marking scheme:
- Correctly explains that Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage, current and resistance: 2 marks.
- Gives the correct formula V = IR or I = V/R: 2 marks.
- Correctly calculates I = 12 / 4 = 3A with the correct unit: 2 marks.

Common mistakes:
- Missing the formula.
- Confusing voltage, current and resistance.
- Correct calculation but missing the unit.
- Using addition instead of division.
