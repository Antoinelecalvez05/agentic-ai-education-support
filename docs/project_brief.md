# Project Brief — Agentic AI for Feedback and Learning Support

Project Description
In Irish universities, increasing student numbers combined with limited academic staffing have significantly reduced the time available for personalised feedback and learning support. As a result, many students receive little or no qualitative feedback on assessed work, making it difficult for them to understand their mistakes, improve their skills, or feel confident in the fairness of grading.
In parallel, project-based learning—particularly in engineering and technical disciplines—often assumes familiarity with specialised tools such as CAD software or circuit simulation environments. Due to limited staff availability, students may receive insufficient guidance on how to approach these tools, which can reduce learning outcomes and limit their potential to fully achieve project objectives.
This project proposes the use of agentic AI as a support tool, both for assessment feedback and for technical project learning. The objective is to improve educational quality, fairness, and equity in Irish higher education, while ensuring that lecturers remain fully in control of all academic decisions.

How Can This Project Help Ireland?
Irish higher education institutions are facing structural challenges linked to staff-to-student ratios, increasing cohort sizes, and growing administrative and teaching workloads. These constraints directly affect the quality and consistency of feedback provided to students and can create inequalities, particularly for students who have not previously been exposed to technical tools or strong academic support networks.
By supporting lecturers with AI-assisted feedback generation and students with guided learning resources, this project helps to:
•	improve fairness and transparency in assessment across large cohorts and multiple graders
•	reduce learning gaps for students from diverse educational backgrounds
•	support Irish universities in maintaining academic standards despite resource constraints
•	strengthen student confidence, progression, and skills development
Overall, the project contributes to Ireland’s broader goals of educational inclusion, skills development, and the responsible use of AI in public-interest domains.

Project Innovation
The innovation of this project lies in the human-in-the-loop use of agentic AI to support, rather than replace, academic work.
Unlike traditional AI tools or generic chatbots, the proposed system:
•	operates using verified marking schemes and lecturer-defined tasks
•	produces personalised feedback and indicative grades that serve as reference points for lecturers
•	compares similar student copies, including those corrected by different graders, to support fair and consistent grading
•	generates learning-support demonstrations based on analogous projects, without providing direct solutions
The agentic approach allows the AI to reason across multiple steps—such as analysis, explanation, comparison, and adaptation—while ensuring that all academic responsibility and final decisions remain with lecturers.

Expected Impacts
•	improved quality and consistency of feedback for students
•	increased fairness and confidence in grading across large cohorts
•	better learning outcomes for students unfamiliar with technical software
•	reduced pressure on academic staff without increasing workload
•	greater equity in access to learning support across Irish universities

Data Specifications
The system relies on well-defined and controlled educational data, used strictly to support learning and assessment transparency.
Primary data sources include:
•	scanned student exam scripts or assignments, processed only after human correction
•	lecturer-provided and verified marking schemes and rubrics
•	anonymised student copies with similar structures or answers, including those corrected by different graders
•	lecturer-defined project briefs, learning objectives, and task constraints
•	software-generated artefacts from example projects, such as CAD files or simulation outputs
Data handling principles include:
•	student data being anonymised or pseudonymised before processing
•	indicative grades generated by the AI not being communicated to students and used only as reference by lecturers
•	example projects for learning support being non-identical to assessed work, preserving academic integrity
•	full compliance with GDPR, institutional data governance policies, and ethical standards
The system is designed to operate within existing university infrastructures, without requiring external datasets or invasive data collection.

AI Systems Framework Specifications
The project is built around an agentic AI architecture, enabling multi-step reasoning and controlled decision flows under lecturer supervision.
Core components include:
•	large language models (LLMs) for natural-language feedback generation and explanation
•	agent orchestration frameworks (e.g. LangChain, AutoGen, CrewAI) to manage sequential tasks such as analysing student answers, applying marking criteria, generating structured feedback, producing indicative grades, and comparing similar copies corrected by different graders
•	retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to ensure that feedback and grading logic are grounded in lecturer-validated marking schemes rather than model assumptions
•	tool-augmented agents for learning support, including step-by-step CAD workflows, circuit simulation demonstrations (e.g. LTSpice), and progressive explanations from 2D to 3D modelling and animation
All AI outputs are designed to be auditable, explainable, and controllable, with lecturers retaining full authority over assessment and instructional decisions.

Project Roadmap and Timeline
Phase 1 – Design and scoping
•	define use cases with lecturers
•	formalise marking-scheme and project-support workflows
Phase 2 – Prototype development
•	build the feedback generation and indicative grading agent
•	build the technical learning support agent (CAD and simulation tools)
Phase 3 – Testing and refinement
•	test with sample assignments and projects
•	validate feedback quality and fairness support with lecturers
Phase 4 – Demonstration and evaluation
•	produce demonstrator outputs (feedback reports and learning-support videos)
•	evaluate educational value and scalability

Novelty (Technical Novelty and Innovation)
The novelty of this project lies in the system-level design of an agentic, RAG-based AI architecture specifically tailored to higher education assessment and learning support.
Rather than proposing a standalone chatbot or an automated grading system, the project combines:
•	retrieval-augmented generation grounded in lecturer-validated marking schemes and course constraints
•	agentic task orchestration enabling sequential reasoning across analysis, comparison, feedback generation, and indicative grading
•	human-in-the-loop control, ensuring that all academic decisions remain with lecturers
A key technical novelty is the use of cross-grader comparison of similar student copies to generate indicative grades as reference points, supporting fairness and consistency in multi-corrector contexts without automating grading outcomes.
In addition, the project introduces a non-solution-based generative approach for project-based learning, where the AI generates demonstrations of analogous projects within the same theme rather than providing direct answers. This preserves academic integrity while enabling scalable, software-specific learning support.
Overall, the innovation lies in the responsible orchestration of existing AI capabilities into a coherent, auditable, and educator-controlled system that addresses structural constraints in Irish higher education.
